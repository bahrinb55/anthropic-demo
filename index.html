Key Economic Insights

⏱️

The Monetary Policy Lag

Rate changes take 12–18 months to fully flow through to inflation. The Fed hiked aggressively in 2022, but CPI didn't peak until June 2022 and didn't fall below 4% until mid-2023.

⚠️

2021: Policy Behind the Curve

The Fed kept rates near zero as CPI surged from 1.4% (Jan 2021) to 7.2% (Dec 2021). This delayed response required far more aggressive hikes later — the fastest tightening cycle since the 1980s.

📉

Negative Real Rates Drive Inflation

When real rates (Fed Funds − CPI) are deeply negative, borrowing is effectively free in real terms, stimulating demand and feeding inflation. 2009–2015 and 2021–2022 both showed this pattern.

✅

2023–2026: Soft Landing

The Fed managed to bring inflation from 9% to ~2.4% without causing a recession — a historically rare outcome. Real rates turned positive in mid-2023 and remained positive through 2025.

🔄

Asymmetric Responses

The Fed cuts quickly in crises (GFC: 5.25% → 0.16% in 15 months; COVID: 1.75% → 0.05% in one month) but hikes slowly in normal times, creating recurring accommodative conditions.

🎯

The 2% Inflation Target

The Fed's 2% CPI target was rarely hit precisely. Inflation spent most of 2012–2020 below target, then dramatically overshot 2021–2022. By early 2026, inflation finally returned near the target zone.

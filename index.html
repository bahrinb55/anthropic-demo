Key Findings & Interpretation

Weak Lagged Correlation

The lagged correlation (r = 0.039, p = 0.554) shows that the Fed rate 12 months prior has no statistically significant linear relationship with current inflation. Monetary policy transmission takes longer than one year and is highly non-linear.

Rates Rise Because of Inflation

The positive contemporaneous correlation (r = 0.304) is largely reverse causation: the Fed raises rates in response to high inflation, not the other way around. Hike months average 4.60% inflation vs. 2.12% during hold months.

ZIRP & Supply Shocks

The 2020–2022 COVID era shows the danger of maintaining near-zero rates while supply shocks drive prices. Inflation climbed to nearly 9% while the Fed held at 0–0.25%, demonstrating that rates alone cannot offset structural supply disruptions.

Aggressive Hikes Eventually Work

After the Fed raised rates from 0.08% (Mar 2022) to 5.33% (Jul 2023), YoY inflation fell from 8.98% to around 3% by mid-2023, then converged toward the 2% target by 2024 — demonstrating that sufficiently aggressive tightening can tame even severe inflation within 18–24 months.
